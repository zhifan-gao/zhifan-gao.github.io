I. Machine Learning and Computer Vision

Image/Video Saliency Detection

Visual saliency detection in visual scenes aims to seek attention of human visual system. It is essential in cognitive psychology and neurobiology that how humans perceive and process the stimuli from sights. Our group has been prompteing the advance of this research field in single-image saliency, co-saliency and video-based saliency.

II. Medical Image Analysis and Clinical Research

Coronary Disease Diagnosis

This research concentrates on automatic image-based diagnosis of varieties of coronary atherosclerosis disease (CAD). Our group proposes computer vision and machine learning methods for CAD analysis on intracoronary different imaging modalities.

  • Context-Aware Inductive Bias Learning for Vessel Border Detection in Multi-modal Intracoronary Imaging. [Link]
    International Conference on Medical Image Computing and Computer Assisted Intervention (MICCAI), 2019.

  • An Artificial Neural Network Method for Lumen and Media-Adventitia Border Detection in IVUS. [Link]
    Computerized Medical Imaging and Graphics, 2017.

  • An Automated Framework for Detecting Lumen and Media-Adventitia Borders in Intravascular Ultrasound Images. [Link]
    Ultrasound in Medicine & Biology, 2015.


Early diagnosis of carotid atherosclerosis disease

This research aims to prompt the early diagnosis and screening of cardiovascular atherosclerosis disease based on artery wall motion in carotid ultrasound imaging. Our group proposed state-of-the-art motion tracking methods. We also studied the clinical physiological relationship between local arterial displacement and beat-to-beat blood pressure.

  • Motion Tracking of the Carotid Artery Wall from Ultrasound Image Sequences: a Nonlinear State-space Approach. [Link]
    IEEE Transactions on Medical Imaging, 2018.

  • Robust Segmentation of Intima-Media Borders with Different Morphologies and Dynamics During the Cardiac Cycle. [Link]
    IEEE Journal of Biomedical and Health Informatics, 2018.

  • Robust Estimation of Carotid Artery Wall Motion Using the Elasticity-Based State-Space Approach. [Link]
    Medical Image Analysis, 2017.

  • Beat-to-Beat Blood Pressure and Two-Dimensional (Axial and Radial) Motion of the Carotid Artery Wall: Physiological Evaluation of Arterial Stiffness. [Link]
    Scientific Reports, 2017.


Contrast-Free Myocardial Infarction Diagnosis

This research focuses on the myocardial infarction diagnosis based on the contrast-free MR imaging. It aims to contribute to the patients who have adverse reactions to contrast material. Our group proposed the detection methods to learn the patterns of myocardial infarction from contrast-free MRI sequences. Moreover, we also validated the feasibility of deep learning technology in chronic myocardial infarction disease.

  • Deep Learning for Diagnosis of Chronic Myocardial Infarction on Nonenhanced Cardiac Cine MRI. [Link]
    Radiology, 2019.

  • Direct Delineation of Myocardial Infarction without Contrast Agents Using a Joint Motion Feature Learning Architecture. [Link]
    Medical Image Analysis, 2018.

  • Direct Detection of Pixel-Level Myocardial Infarction Areas via a Deep-Learning Algorithm. [Link]
    International Conference on Medical Image Computing and Computer Assisted Intervention (MICCAI), 2017.
